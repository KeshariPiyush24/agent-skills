# Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025

Notified by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) vide G.S.R. 846(E), Gazette of India Extraordinary, Part II, Section 3(i), 13 November 2025. Corrigenda G.S.R. 892(E) of 10 December 2025 have been applied to this text. Made under Section 40(1)-(2) of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (22 of 2023).

## Commencement (Rule 1)

- (1) These rules may be called the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025.
- (2) Rules 1, 2 and 17 to 21 came into force on publication (13 November 2025).
- (3) Rule 4 (Consent Manager registration) comes into force one year after publication (13 November 2026).
- (4) Rules 3, 5 to 16, 22 and 23 come into force eighteen months after publication (13 May 2027).

## Rule 2 — Definitions

(1) In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires:

- (a) "Act" means the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (22 of 2023);
- (b) "techno-legal measures" means as referred to under rules 20 and 22;
- (c) "user account" means the online account registered by the Data Principal with the Data Fiduciary, and includes any profiles, pages, handles, email address, mobile number and other similar presences by means of which such Data Principal is able to access the services of such Data Fiduciary; and
- (d) "verifiable consent" means a consent as specified in rule 10 or 11.

(2) Words and expressions used in these rules and not defined, but defined in the Act, have the meanings assigned in the Act.

## Rule 3 — Notice given by Data Fiduciary to Data Principal

The notice given by the Data Fiduciary to the Data Principal shall:

- (a) be presented and be understandable independently of any other information that has been, is or may be made available by such Data Fiduciary;
- (b) give, in clear and plain language, a fair account of the details necessary to enable the Data Principal to give specific and informed consent for the processing of her personal data, including at minimum:
  - (i) an itemised description of such personal data; and
  - (ii) the specified purpose or purposes of, and specific description of the goods or services to be provided or uses to be enabled by, such processing; and
- (c) give the particular communication link for accessing the website or app, or both, of such Data Fiduciary, and a description of other means, if any, using which such Data Principal may:
  - (i) withdraw her consent, with the ease of doing so being comparable to that with which such consent was given;
  - (ii) exercise her rights under the Act; and
  - (iii) make a complaint to the Board.

## Rule 4 — Registration and obligations of Consent Manager

1. A person fulfilling the conditions in Part A of the First Schedule may apply to the Board for registration as a Consent Manager, furnishing particulars, information and documents as the Board publishes on its website.
2. On receipt, the Board may inquire as it deems fit; if satisfied, it registers the applicant (with intimation, and publication of particulars on its website); if not satisfied, it rejects the application with reasons.
3. The Consent Manager shall have obligations as specified in Part B of the First Schedule.
4. If the Board is of the opinion a Consent Manager is not adhering to the conditions and obligations, it may, after giving an opportunity of being heard, inform the Consent Manager and direct it to take measures to ensure adherence.
5. The Board may, if satisfied it is necessary in the interests of Data Principals, after an opportunity of being heard, by order with recorded reasons: (a) suspend or cancel the registration; and (b) give directions to protect the interests of Data Principals.
6. The Board may require the Consent Manager to furnish information.

## Rule 5 — Processing by State and its instrumentalities (subsidy, benefit, service, certificate, licence or permit)

1. Processing personal data under this rule shall follow the standards specified in the Second Schedule.
2. References to a subsidy, benefit, service, certificate, licence or permit provided or issued:
   - (a) "under law": in exercise of any power or performance of any function by the State or its instrumentalities under any law in force;
   - (b) "under policy": under any policy or instruction issued by the Central or a State Government in exercise of executive power; and
   - (c) "using public funds": by incurring expenditure from, or with accrual of receipts to, the Consolidated Fund of India or of a State or the public accounts, or the funds of any local or other authority.

## Rule 6 — Reasonable security safeguards

(1) A Data Fiduciary shall protect personal data in its possession or under its control, including processing by a Data Processor on its behalf, by taking reasonable security safeguards to prevent personal data breach, including at minimum:

- (a) appropriate data security measures, such as securing personal data through encryption, obfuscation, masking or virtual tokens mapped to that personal data;
- (b) appropriate measures to control access to the computer resources used by the Data Fiduciary or Data Processor;
- (c) visibility on the accessing of such personal data, through appropriate logs, monitoring and review, enabling detection of unauthorised access, its investigation and remediation to prevent recurrence;
- (d) reasonable measures for continued processing in the event of compromise of confidentiality, integrity or availability (e.g. destruction or loss of access), such as data backups;
- (e) retention of such logs and personal data for a period of one year (for detection, investigation, remediation and continuity), unless another law requires otherwise;
- (f) appropriate provisions in the contract between Data Fiduciary and Data Processor for taking reasonable security safeguards; and
- (g) appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure effective observance of security safeguards.

(2) "Computer resource" has the meaning assigned in the Information Technology Act, 2000 (21 of 2000).

## Rule 7 — Intimation of personal data breach

(1) On becoming aware of any personal data breach, the Data Fiduciary shall, to the best of its knowledge, intimate each affected Data Principal, in a concise, clear and plain manner and **without delay**, through her user account or any registered mode of communication:

- (a) a description of the breach, including its nature, extent and timing;
- (b) the consequences relevant to her likely to arise from the breach;
- (c) the measures implemented and being implemented, if any, to mitigate risk;
- (d) the safety measures she may take to protect her interests; and
- (e) business contact information of a person able to respond to her queries on behalf of the Data Fiduciary.

(2) On becoming aware of any personal data breach, the Data Fiduciary shall intimate the **Board**:

- (a) **without delay**: a description of the breach, including its nature, extent, timing and location of occurrence and the likely impact;
- (b) **within seventy-two hours** of becoming aware (or such longer period as the Board may allow on written request):
  - (i) updated and detailed information in respect of such description;
  - (ii) the broad facts related to the events, circumstances and reasons leading to the breach;
  - (iii) measures implemented or proposed, if any, to mitigate risk;
  - (iv) any findings regarding the person who caused the breach;
  - (v) remedial measures taken to prevent recurrence; and
  - (vi) a report regarding the intimations given to affected Data Principals.

## Rule 8 — Time period for specified purpose to be deemed no longer served (erasure)

1. A Data Fiduciary of a class specified in the Third Schedule, processing personal data for the corresponding purposes, shall **erase** such personal data — unless retention is necessary for compliance with any law — on expiry of the corresponding time period, if the Data Principal neither approaches the Data Fiduciary for the specified purpose nor exercises her rights in relation to such processing.
2. **At least forty-eight hours before** the erasure period completes, the Data Fiduciary shall inform the Data Principal that the data will be erased unless she logs into her user account or otherwise initiates contact or exercises her rights.
3. Without prejudice to (1) and (2), the Data Fiduciary shall retain, for any processing undertaken by it or its Data Processor, the personal data, associated traffic data and other processing logs for a **minimum of one year** from the date of processing, for the purposes specified in the Seventh Schedule, after which it shall cause the data and logs to be erased unless further retention is required by law or notified by the Government.

Illustrations: (Case 1) An e-book platform must retain order details, personal data and processing logs (order confirmation, payment, delivery events) for at least one year from the transaction even if the user deletes her account. (Case 2) A company using a cloud provider as Data Processor must ensure the processor also retains data and logs for at least one year before erasure.

## Rule 9 — Contact information for questions about processing

Every Data Fiduciary shall prominently publish on its website or app, and mention in every response to a communication for the exercise of Data Principal rights, the business contact information of the Data Protection Officer (if applicable) or a person able to answer questions about the processing of her personal data on behalf of the Data Fiduciary.

## Rule 10 — Verifiable consent for processing personal data of a child

(1) A Data Fiduciary shall adopt appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure **verifiable consent of the parent is obtained before processing any personal data of a child**, and shall observe due diligence to check that the individual identifying herself as the parent is an adult who is identifiable if required under law, by reference to:

- (a) reliable details of identity and age available with the Data Fiduciary; or
- (b) details of identity and age voluntarily provided (i) by the individual, or (ii) through a virtual token mapped to such details, issued by an authorised entity.

(2) Definitions: "adult" = an individual who has completed eighteen years; "authorised entity" = an entity entrusted by law or by the Central/State Government with issuance of identity/age details or a mapped virtual token (or a person appointed/permitted by such entity), and includes details made available and verified by a Digital Locker service provider; "Digital Locker service provider" = an intermediary notified by the Central Government under rules made under the IT Act, 2000.

Illustration cases: whether the parent is already a registered user (check held identity/age details) or not (check against details issued by an entrusted entity or a virtual token, e.g. via Digital Locker) — in all four cases the Data Fiduciary must confirm the parent is an identifiable adult before processing the child's data.

## Rule 11 — Verifiable consent for a person with disability who has a lawful guardian

(1) When obtaining verifiable consent from an individual identifying as the lawful guardian of a person with disability, the Data Fiduciary shall observe due diligence to verify that such guardian is appointed by a court of law, a designated authority (Section 15, Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016) or a local level committee (Section 13, National Trust Act, 1999) under the applicable guardianship law.

(2) Defines "designated authority", "law applicable to guardianship", "local level committee" and "person with disability" by reference to the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 (49 of 2016) and the National Trust for the Welfare of Persons with Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation and Multiple Disabilities Act, 1999 (44 of 1999).

## Rule 12 — Exemptions from certain child-data obligations

1. Section 9(1) and 9(3) of the Act do not apply to processing of a child's personal data by the classes of Data Fiduciaries specified in Part A of the Fourth Schedule, subject to the conditions there.
2. Section 9(1) and 9(3) do not apply to processing for the purposes specified in Part B of the Fourth Schedule, subject to the conditions there.

## Rule 13 — Additional obligations of Significant Data Fiduciary

1. An SDF shall, once in every twelve-month period from the date it is notified as an SDF or is included in a class of Data Fiduciaries notified as such, undertake a **Data Protection Impact Assessment and an audit** to ensure effective observance of the Act and rules.
2. The SDF shall cause the person carrying out the DPIA and audit to furnish to the Board a **report containing significant observations**.
3. The SDF shall observe due diligence to verify that technical measures, including **algorithmic software**, adopted for hosting, display, uploading, modification, publishing, transmission, storage, updating or sharing of personal data are **not likely to pose a risk to the rights of Data Principals**.
4. The SDF shall ensure that personal data **specified by the Central Government** (on a committee's recommendation) is processed subject to the restriction that the personal data and traffic data pertaining to its flow **is not transferred outside the territory of India**.
5. "Committee" means a committee constituted by the Central Government for this rule, including officials from the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology *(sic — the Gazette prints "Ministry of Electronics and Technology"; not corrected by the corrigenda)* and possibly officials from other Ministries or Departments of the Central Government.

## Rule 14 — Rights of Data Principals

1. To enable exercise of rights, the Data Fiduciary and, where applicable, the Consent Manager shall prominently publish on website/app: (a) details of the means for making a rights request; and (b) the particulars (e.g. username or other identifier) needed to identify the Data Principal under its terms of service.
2. The Data Principal may make a request to the Data Fiduciary to whom she previously gave consent, using the required means and particulars.
3. Every Data Fiduciary and Consent Manager shall prominently publish, under its **grievance redressal system**, a response period **not exceeding ninety days** for responding to grievances, and shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure effectiveness. *(Note: the Gazette text of Rule 14(3) is grammatically defective — "publish" lacks an object — and was not corrected by G.S.R. 892(E); this paraphrase reflects the evident intent that the published grievance-response period must not exceed ninety days.)*
4. The Data Principal may, per the Data Fiduciary's terms of service and applicable law, **nominate one or more individuals** to exercise her rights.
5. "Identifier" = any sequence of characters issued by the Data Fiduciary to identify the Data Principal (customer identification file number, customer acquisition form number, application reference number, enrolment ID, email address, mobile number, licence number, etc.).

## Rule 15 — Transfer of personal data outside India

Personal data processed by a Data Fiduciary under the Act may be transferred outside the territory of India, subject to the restriction that the Data Fiduciary shall meet such requirements as the Central Government may, by general or special order, specify in respect of making such personal data available to any foreign State, or to any person or entity under the control of, or any agency of, such a State.

## Rule 16 — Exemption for research, archiving or statistical purposes

The Act does not apply to processing of personal data necessary for research, archiving or statistical purposes if carried on in accordance with the standards specified in the Second Schedule.

## Rules 17-21 — Board machinery (in force since 13 Nov 2025)

- **Rule 17**: Search-cum-Selection Committees for appointing the Chairperson (chaired by Cabinet Secretary) and other Members (chaired by MeitY Secretary); Central Government appoints after considering recommendations; proceedings not invalid for vacancy/defect.
- **Rule 18**: Salary, allowances and terms of service of Chairperson and Members are as in the Fifth Schedule.
- **Rule 19**: Board meeting procedure — Chairperson fixes date/time/place and agenda; quorum is one-third of membership; majority vote with casting vote for the chair; conflicted Members must not participate or vote; emergency action by the Chairperson (with reasons recorded in writing), to be communicated to all Members within seven days and ratified at the next meeting; decision by circulation permitted; orders/directions/instruments authenticated under signature. **Rule 19(9): the Board's inquiry shall be completed within six months** of receipt of the intimation, complaint, reference or direction under Section 27, extendable by recorded reasons for up to three months at a time.
- **Rule 20**: The Board functions as a **digital office**; it may adopt techno-legal measures to conduct proceedings without physical presence, without prejudice to its power to summon and examine on oath.
- **Rule 21**: The Board may appoint officers and employees with Central Government approval; terms per the Sixth Schedule.

## Rule 22 — Appeal to Appellate Tribunal

1. Any person aggrieved by an order or direction of the Board may appeal to the Appellate Tribunal (TDSAT); the appeal shall be filed in digital form as the Tribunal decides.
2. Fee is the same as for appeals under the TRAI Act, 1997, unless reduced or waived by the Tribunal's Chairperson; payable digitally via UPI or another RBI-authorised payment system.
3. The Tribunal is not bound by the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 but is guided by natural justice and may regulate its own procedure; it functions as a **digital office** and may adopt **techno-legal measures** to conduct proceedings without requiring the physical presence of any individual (Rule 22(3)(b) — one of the two anchors, with Rule 20, for the Rule 2(1)(b) definition).

## Rule 23 — Calling for information from Data Fiduciary or intermediary

1. The Central Government may, for the purposes specified in the Seventh Schedule, acting through the corresponding authorised person, require any Data Fiduciary or intermediary to furnish information within the period given in such order.
2. Where disclosure would prejudicially affect the sovereignty and integrity of India or security of the State, the Government may require the Data Fiduciary or intermediary **not to disclose** the furnishing of information to the affected Data Principal or any other person except with prior written permission of the authorised person.
3. "Intermediary" has the meaning assigned in the IT Act, 2000.

# Schedules

## First Schedule (see Rule 4) — Consent Managers

### Part A — Conditions for registration

1. Applicant is a company incorporated in India.
2. Sufficient technical, operational and financial capacity.
3. Sound financial condition and general character of management.
4. **Net worth not less than two crore rupees.**
5. Adequate likely business volume, capital structure and earning prospects.
6. Directors, key managerial personnel (KMP) and senior management have a general reputation and record of fairness and integrity.
7. Memorandum and articles of association contain provisions requiring adherence to items 9 and 10 of Part B (conflict-of-interest obligations), with policies/procedures for adherence, amendable only with prior Board approval.
8. Proposed operations are in the interests of Data Principals.
9. Independent certification that: (a) the **interoperable platform** for give/manage/review/withdraw consent is consistent with the data protection standards and assurance framework published by the Board; and (b) appropriate technical and organisational measures ensure adherence and effective observance of the transparency obligations in item 11 of Part B.

### Part B — Obligations of Consent Manager

1. Enable a Data Principal to give consent to processing by an onboarded Data Fiduciary, either directly or routed through another onboarded Data Fiduciary that maintains the data with her consent (e.g. account-aggregator-style flows between banks).
2. Ensure the manner of making available or sharing personal data is such that **its contents are not readable by the Consent Manager**.
3. Maintain a record of: (a) consents given, denied or withdrawn; (b) notices preceding or accompanying consent requests; and (c) sharing of personal data with transferee Data Fiduciaries.
4. Give the Data Principal access to that record; provide it in machine-readable form on request; **retain the record for at least seven years** (or longer by agreement or law).
5. Maintain a website or app (or both) as the primary means of access to its services.
6. **Not sub-contract or assign** performance of its obligations.
7. Take reasonable security safeguards to prevent personal data breach.
8. Act in a **fiduciary capacity** in relation to the Data Principal.
9. Avoid conflict of interest with Data Fiduciaries, including in respect of promoters and KMP.
10. Have measures ensuring no conflict of interest arises from directors/KMP/senior management holding directorships, financial interests, employment or beneficial ownership in Data Fiduciaries, or material pecuniary relationships with them.
11. Publish in an easily accessible manner on website/app: (a) promoters, directors, KMP and senior management; (b) every person holding more than 2% shareholding of the Consent Manager; (c) every body corporate in whose shareholding any promoter/director/KMP/senior management of the Consent Manager holds more than 2% as on the first day of the preceding calendar month; and (d) other information the Board directs in the interests of transparency.
12. Have effective audit mechanisms to review, monitor, evaluate and report to the Board periodically (and as directed) on technical and organisational controls, continued fulfilment of registration conditions, and adherence to obligations.
13. Control of the company shall not be transferred (sale, merger or otherwise) without prior Board approval and fulfilment of conditions the Board specifies.

Note: "body corporate", "company", "control", "director", "key managerial personnel", "promoter" and "senior management" take their Companies Act, 2013 (18 of 2013) meanings; "net worth" means total assets minus liabilities per books of accounts.

## Second Schedule (see Rules 5(1) and 16) — Standards for State processing under Section 7(b) and for research/archiving/statistics under Section 17(2)(b)

Appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure, in accordance with applicable law:

- (a) processing is lawful;
- (b) processing is for the uses in Section 7(b) or purposes in Section 17(2)(b), as applicable;
- (c) processing is limited to personal data necessary for those uses/purposes;
- (d) reasonable efforts to ensure completeness, accuracy and consistency of personal data;
- (e) retention only as long as required for those uses/purposes or legal compliance;
- (f) reasonable security safeguards to prevent personal data breach, including for processing by a Data Processor;
- (g) for Section 7(b) processing: intimation to the Data Principal, with (i) business contact information of a person able to answer questions, (ii) the communication link (website/app) and other means for exercising rights, and (iii) consistency with other applicable standards under Government policy or law; and
- (h) accountability of the person who determines the purpose and means of processing.

## Third Schedule (see Rule 8(1)) — Data erasure time periods

| S. no. | Class of Data Fiduciaries | Purposes | Time period |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | E-commerce entity with at least 2 crore (20 million) registered users in India | All purposes, except (a) enabling access to her user account and (b) enabling access to any virtual token issued by/for the Data Fiduciary, stored on its platform, usable for money, goods or services | Three years from the date the Data Principal last approached the Data Fiduciary for the specified purpose or exercise of rights, or commencement of these Rules, whichever is latest |
| 2 | Online gaming intermediary with at least 50 lakh (5 million) registered users in India | Same exceptions as above | Same three-year period |
| 3 | Social media intermediary with at least 2 crore (20 million) registered users in India | Same exceptions as above | Same three-year period |

Note: "e-commerce entity" per the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (excluding sellers on a marketplace); "online gaming intermediary" enables access to online games; "social media intermediary" per the IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021. **"user"** (for the registered-user thresholds): for an e-commerce entity — any person who accesses or avails any computer resource of that entity; for an online gaming or social media intermediary — any person who accesses or avails the intermediary's computer resource for hosting, publishing, sharing, transacting, viewing, displaying, downloading or uploading information.

## Fourth Schedule (see Rule 12) — Exemptions from Section 9(1) and 9(3) for children's data

### Part A — Exempt classes of Data Fiduciaries (with conditions)

1. Clinical establishment, mental health establishment or healthcare professional — restricted to provision of health services to the child, to the extent necessary for protection of her health.
2. Allied healthcare professional — restricted to supporting implementation of a treatment and referral plan for the child, to the extent necessary for protection of her health.
3. Educational institution — restricted to tracking and behavioural monitoring for educational activities or in the interests of safety of enrolled children.
4. Individual caring for infants/children in a crèche or child day care centre — restricted to tracking and behavioural monitoring in the interests of children's safety.
5. Person engaged by an educational institution/crèche/child care centre for transport of children — restricted to location tracking during travel to and from the institution, in the interests of safety.

### Part B — Exempt purposes (with conditions)

1. Exercise of any power/function/duty in the interests of a child under any law in force in India — to the extent necessary.
2. Providing/issuing any subsidy, benefit, service, certificate, licence or permit in the interests of a child under Section 7(b) — to the extent necessary.
3. Creation of a user account for communicating by email — restricted to what is necessary; use limited to email communication.
4. Determination of real-time location of a child — restricted to tracking in the interest of her safety and protection or security.
5. Ensuring that information, services or advertisements likely to have a detrimental effect on a child's well-being are not accessible to her — to the extent necessary.
6. Confirmation that the Data Principal is not a child and due-diligence observance under Rule 10 — to the extent necessary.

Note (as re-lettered by the corrigenda, items (a) to (g)): "advertisement" per the Consumer Protection Act, 2019; "allied healthcare professional" and "healthcare professional" and "health services" per the National Commission for Allied and Healthcare Professions Act, 2021; "clinical establishment" per the Clinical Establishments (Registration and Regulation) Act, 2010; "educational institution" means an institution of learning that imparts education, including vocational education; "mental health establishment" per the Mental Healthcare Act, 2017.

## Fifth Schedule (see Rule 18) — Terms of service of Chairperson and Members

Chairperson: consolidated salary of Rs 4,50,000 per month (no house/car facility); Members: Rs 4,00,000 per month. Provident fund contribution eligible; no pension or gratuity; travel/medical/leave/LTC benefits pegged to Central Government pay-matrix levels 17 (Chairperson) and 15 (Members); conflict-of-interest bar; CCS (CCA) Rules Parts IV-IX apply mutatis mutandis; no sitting fee or sumptuary allowance; residual matters referred to the Central Government.

## Sixth Schedule (see Rule 21(2)) — Officers and employees of the Board

Appointment on deputation (up to five years) from Central/State Government, autonomous/statutory bodies or public sector enterprises; deputation also possible from the National Institute for Smart Government with market-standard pay; gratuity per the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972; travel, medical, leave, LTC and conduct/discipline rules mirror Central Government service rules; residual matters referred to the Central Government.

## Seventh Schedule (see Rules 23(1) and 8(3)) — Purposes and authorised persons for calling information

| S. no. | Purpose | Authorised person |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Use by the State or its instrumentalities of a Data Principal's personal data in the interest of sovereignty and integrity of India or security of the State | Officer of the State/instrumentality notified under Section 17(2)(a), designated by the Central Government or head of the instrumentality |
| 2 | Use by the State or its instrumentalities for (i) performance of any function under any law in force in India, or (ii) disclosure of information to fulfil any legal obligation | Person authorised under applicable law |
| 3 | Assessment for notifying any Data Fiduciary or class as Significant Data Fiduciary | Officer of the Central Government in MeitY designated by the Secretary in charge |
